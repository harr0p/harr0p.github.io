Firm-Pair Merger Prediction in Technology and Bio Tech Sectors: A Topological and Machine Learning Approach
Python · Group project · April 2026

Built an M&A prediction pipeline embedding patent portfolios of 7,485 firms via PatentSBERTa and Bayesian Gaussian Mixture Models, then measured technological synergy between firm pairs using the Bhattacharyya Coefficient and a directional complementarity metric, Comp(A → B), that identifies whether a target fills an acquirer's market-relative R&D gaps. Validated the framework against real M&A case studies (AMD/Xilinx, Analog Devices/Maxim, Merck/Acceleron, HP/Poly), correctly distinguishing capability acquisitions from strategic mimicry, and confirmed external validity with a 0.99 correlation to traditional patent citation overlap across 1,000 firm pairs. Extended the framework into a predictive logistic regression on 46 historical M&A events, achieving a Hit@5 of 0.72 against a 23.8% random baseline.

Undergraduate Thesis — "Are the Markets Just Bandwagon Fans? Recency Bias in NBA Betting Markets"
R · Supervised by Prof. Miguel Ortiz · ECON 490

Tested whether NBA betting markets efficiently price recent team performance using over 35,000 games from 2007 to 2023, comparing market-implied win probabilities (from de-vigged moneyline odds) against a benchmark logit model of true win probability, with team fixed effects and clustered standard errors. Found that markets respond to a one-point increase in recent win rate about two to three times more strongly than is actually justified by game outcomes, a gap that is statistically significant and stable across the full sample period. The bias is asymmetric: it is concentrated in underdogs and driven more by losing streaks than winning streaks, consistent with loss-averse belief updating rather than uniform mispricing.

Data Cleaning and Analysis of Health Insurance Coverage in Kenya
Python · with Jesse DeCoste · UBC · Spring 2023

Cleaned and spatially merged Kenya Demographic and Health Survey data from 2008 and 2014 with subcounty shapefiles to examine public health funding discrepancies before and after Kenya's 2010 devolution of health governance to counties, with a focus on Wajir province. Mapped county- and subcounty-level changes in health insurance coverage, finding gains concentrated in rural counties north of Nairobi and the Rift Valley alongside counterintuitive declines in the urban centers of Nairobi and Mombasa. Fit logistic regressions on the underlying survey microdata to identify individual-level determinants of coverage, isolating age, wealth, and education as the strongest predictors.
