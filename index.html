About Me

I am currently a Research Assistant at the Vancouver School of Economics, UBC, working with Professor Miguel Ortiz on a multi-wave randomized controlled trial studying the effects of Chinese and American business-training programs on worker productivity among firms in Addis Ababa, Ethiopia, in collaboration with Professor David Wu (Georgia State University).

I recently completed a B.A. in Economics and Statistics, with a minor in Data Science, at the University of British Columbia. Before returning to research, I worked as an investment analyst in London at Arbion and Union Bancaire Privée.

My research interests center on applied microeconomics and development economics: causal inference in field experiments, information efficiency in markets, and behavioral bias in decision-making. My undergraduate thesis examined recency bias in NBA betting markets using data from over 35,000 games.

Looking ahead, I am applying to predoctoral research positions and, eventually, PhD programs in economics.

Undergraduate Thesis — "Are the Markets Just Bandwagon Fans? Recency Bias in NBA Betting Markets"
Supervised by Prof. Miguel Ortiz · ECON 490

Empirical analysis of over 35,000 NBA games (2007–2023) using a three-model design (market model, benchmark logit, bias model) with team fixed effects and clustered standard errors. Finds betting markets overweight recent performance by 2–3x the justified amount, an effect concentrated in underdogs.
